## 🚀 What Is SwiftGMessages?

SwiftGMessages is a native app designed for macOS, iOS, and visionOS. It pairs with your Android phone through Google Messages. Once linked, it syncs your messages in real-time to your Mac or iPad. You can send texts, attach photos, record voice messages, and get notifications just like you would on your phone. The app also supports advanced messaging features like read receipts, message reactions, and link previews.

### Key Features

- **Phone Pairing**: Quickly link your Android phone by scanning a QR code.
- **Real-Time Sync**: Your conversations update instantly.
- **Send & Receive**: Texts, images, videos, and voice notes.
- **Reactions & Read Receipts**: React to messages and see who has read them.
- **Push Notifications**: Get alerts for new messages with customizable options.
- **Background Sync**: Keeps conversations updated even when the app isn’t open.
- **Conversation Management**: Search, archive, mute, and organize your messages.
- **Link Previews**: See a summary when someone sends a link.
- **Multi-Platform Support**: Works on macOS, iOS, and visionOS devices natively.

## 💻 System Requirements

To use SwiftGMessages, make sure your devices meet these requirements:

- **macOS** version 26.1 or later, or
- **iOS** version 26.1 or later (also supports visionOS 26.1+).
- **Xcode 16** or newer (for building or advanced use, not needed to run the app).
- An **Android phone** with Google Messages installed and turned on for web access.
- A stable Wi-Fi or cellular connection.
- Camera access on your Mac or iPad to scan the QR code for pairing.

## 🔗 Pair Your Android Phone

To link your phone:

1. Open **Google Messages** on your Android phone.
2. Tap the **three-dot menu** in the top-right corner.
3. Select **"Device pairing"** or **"Messages for web"**.
4. Tap **“QR code scanner.”**
5. On SwiftGMessages, the app will display a QR code. Use your phone to scan this code.
6. Your phone will confirm the connection. Once paired, your conversations will appear on your Mac or iPad.

## 🛠 Troubleshooting Tips

- If messages don’t sync, make sure your phone and Mac/iPad are connected to the internet.
- Reboot the app and try the QR code pairing step again.
- Check that Google Messages is set as your default SMS app on your phone.
- Make sure your phone isn’t in battery saver mode that blocks background data.
- Confirm app permissions for camera (to scan QR codes) and notifications.

## 🔐 Privacy & Security
